GREAT MINDS DURING THE AGE OF ENLIGHTENMENT

America's unique government unprecedentedly gave power to the people. Federalism serves as the keystone, enabling all 50 states to coexist harmoniously even through hard times. While you may have heard of the Founding Fathers who wrote the U.S Constitution, did you know that their ideas were not entirely original? Other countries have drafted similar government documents in its likeness because they recognize its capacity for developing a strong society. When the Constitution was crafted, its authors drew heavily from the ideas of Enlightenment (Age of Reason) philosophers. Through them, the Founding Fathers were inspired to create a government that ensures justice, safety, and appropriate powers.

LOCKE

BARON DE MONTESQUIEU

A French political philosopher known for his theory of the separation of powers, which has greatly influenced modern democratic governance.

JEAN-JAQUES ROUSSEAU

A French philosopher who influenced the Enlightenment and modern political thought with his works on the social contract and the nature of human freedom.
