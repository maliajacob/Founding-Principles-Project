The Social Contract is the implied agreement between members of a community to abide by the rules. These rules are put into place in order to achieve a greater well being of society. An earlier version of the Social Contract created systems and outlined the government. However, an updated version ceasing those limits was needed in order for it to gain popularity and functionality. The general will of the people is described as the will of all, and then substracting the parts where individual wills clash. One aspect of government that uses the social contract is paying taxes. In the US today, people pay taxes in which then benefit them: highways, post offices, and public education.
