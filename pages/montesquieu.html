There are three types of government: republican, monarchies, and despotism. Each have their own weaknesses that could make them corrupt, and each need to create some kind of motivation in their people in order to keep functioning. For example, a despotic may do whatever he pleases, without reason, without discussion; and what that despotic instills in his people is fear. All punishment which is not derived from necessity is tyrannical. The law is not a mere act of power; things in their own nature indifferent are not within its province. Unlike the other types of government, democracy has virtue-the love of the laws and of our country. Voting, sufferage, and social contract are fundemental to this type of government. Everyone needs the opportunity to thrive in order to not have self-seekers. "Demoracies can become corrupt in two ways: the spirit of inequality and the spirit of extreme equality" The core principles of a good government are described as human passions set in motion. These laws too can become corrupt. Constant experience shows us that every man invested with power is apt to abuse it ... it is necessary from the very nature of things that power should be a check to power. This is done throughout the separation of powers. Having three branches of government with checks and balances creates a harmonious system.
